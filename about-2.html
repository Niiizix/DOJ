DOJ Agencies

The Department of Justice operates through various specialized agencies, each with distinct responsibilities in law enforcement, investigation, and public safety.

Law Enforcement Agencies

Our law enforcement agencies form the backbone of federal crime prevention and investigation efforts across the United States. These specialized units work collaboratively to address threats ranging from organized crime to cybersecurity breaches.

The Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI) serves as the premier federal investigative agency, while the Drug Enforcement Administration (DEA) focuses specifically on narcotics-related crimes. The Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives (ATF) handles firearms violations and explosive incidents.

Each agency maintains specialized task forces, forensic laboratories, and training facilities to ensure agents have the resources necessary to protect American citizens and uphold federal law.

Support & Administrative Agencies

Behind every successful law enforcement operation is a network of support agencies that provide critical administrative, legal, and logistical services. These components ensure smooth operations across all DOJ initiatives.

The U.S. Marshals Service protects federal courts, apprehends fugitives, and manages the federal witness protection program. The Federal Prison System operates correctional facilities nationwide, while the Office of Justice Programs coordinates grant funding and research initiatives.

Legal divisions provide expert counsel on constitutional matters, civil rights enforcement, and federal prosecution standards. Together, these agencies create a comprehensive framework for justice administration at the federal level.
