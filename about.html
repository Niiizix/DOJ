Our Mission

The United States Department of Justice serves to enforce the law and defend the interests of the United States according to the law; to ensure public safety against threats foreign and domestic; to provide federal leadership in preventing and controlling crime; to seek just punishment for those guilty of unlawful behavior; and to ensure fair and impartial administration of justice for all Americans.

We are committed to upholding the Constitution and the rule of law, protecting civil rights, and ensuring that justice is accessible to all citizens regardless of their background or circumstances.

Leadership

The Department of Justice is led by the Attorney General, who serves as the chief law enforcement officer of the United States. The Attorney General is appointed by the President and confirmed by the Senate.

Our leadership team includes experienced attorneys, investigators, and public servants dedicated to upholding justice and protecting the American people. Together, we work to ensure that the Department's mission is carried out effectively and efficiently.

DOJ History Timeline

1870

Department Established

The Department of Justice was established by Congress to handle the legal business of the United States.

1908

FBI Founded

The Federal Bureau of Investigation was created as the investigative arm of the Department of Justice.

1973

DEA Established

The Drug Enforcement Administration was formed to combat the growing drug problem in America.

2001

Post-9/11 Reforms

Major restructuring to focus on counterterrorism and national security threats.
